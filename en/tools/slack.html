About

Slack is a messaging platform for team communication, organized around channels — topic-focused spaces where conversations, files, and notifications live. Direct messages, group chats, and Huddle voice/video calls keep teams connected. With 2,000+ app integrations (GitHub, Jira, Google Drive, etc.), it becomes a central hub for work notifications.

Key Features

  • 💬 Channel-based Messaging
  • 📁 File Sharing
  • 🎧 Huddle (Voice/Video)
  • 🔔 App Notification Hub
  • 🔌 2000+ Integrations
  • 🔍 Message Search
  • 📱 Mobile App
  • 🤖 Workflow Builder

💡 Tips

  • Bookmark important messages with the save icon — find them later in your 'Saved Items' for quick reference.
  • Use the /remind command to set a reminder for any message or task so it resurfaces at the right time.
  • Define channel naming conventions (e.g. #proj-name, #team-name) early — it makes workspace navigation much cleaner as your team grows.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Slack free?

The free plan provides 90 days of message history and 10 app integrations. Unlimited history and advanced features require Pro at $7.25/user/month.

Should we replace email with Slack?

Slack is faster for internal team communication. Email is still standard for formal external communications with clients and partners.

Can we do video calls in Slack?

Huddle supports audio and video calls. For large meetings, integrating Zoom or Google Meet is more practical.

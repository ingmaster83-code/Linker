About

Naver News is South Korea's dominant news aggregator, operated by Naver. It brings together articles from hundreds of Korean media outlets — including major newspapers, broadcasters, and digital outlets — into a single interface. Features include real-time trending rankings, topic-based sections (Politics, Economy, Society, Tech), and reader comments. Naver News is the first stop for most Koreans seeking domestic news.

💡 Tips

  • Set up your News Stand by subscribing to your favourite outlets — their top stories will appear on your Naver homepage.
  • Leave "Not interested" feedback on topics you dislike to help the algorithm refine your personalised feed.
  • Register interest keywords in the "My News" tab of the Naver app to receive a custom news feed matching your interests.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Naver News available in English?

The interface and articles are in Korean. For English-language Korean news, try Korea JoongAng Daily or The Korea Herald.

Do I need an account to read articles?

No — all articles are freely accessible without an account. A Naver account is only required to post comments.

How does the real-time ranking work?

The ranking reflects the most-read articles across all Naver News outlets in the past hour, updated in real time. It's useful for quickly spotting breaking news.

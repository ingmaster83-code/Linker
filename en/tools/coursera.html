About

Coursera was founded by Stanford professors and partners with 350+ universities and companies — including Google, IBM, Meta, and Deloitte — to deliver official online courses. Most courses can be audited free; paid enrollment unlocks assignments and completion certificates. Google Career Certificates, IBM Professional Certificates, and even full online degrees from top universities are available through the platform.

💡 Tips

  • To study without paying, look for the "Audit" option on the course enrollment page — most lecture videos are freely accessible this way.
  • Google Career Certificates (Data Analytics, UX Design, IT Support, etc.) are completable in 6 months and are recognised by hundreds of hiring companies as evidence of practical skills.
  • Coursera Plus ($59/mo or ~$399/year) gives unlimited access to 7,000+ courses and certificates — worth it if you plan to take several courses simultaneously.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take courses for free?

Yes — most courses offer a free "Audit" mode for watching lectures. Graded assignments and certificates require paid enrollment or a Coursera Plus subscription.

Are Google Career Certificates valuable?

Google Career Certificates are increasingly recognised by employers, particularly in IT support, data analytics, project management, and UX design. They signal practical, job-ready skills.

Can I earn a real degree on Coursera?

Yes. Coursera hosts accredited online master's degree programs from universities like the University of Illinois and University of Michigan. Graduates receive the same diploma as on-campus students.
